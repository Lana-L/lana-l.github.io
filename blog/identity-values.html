Identity and Values

The aim of this blog is to reflect on your core personal values, and your strengths and limitations.

Your blog post should include the following:

  1. Explain a situation where you have made an ethical decision. Discuss how you weighed up the values involved in that decision, the decision you made, and how you reflect on the decision now.
  2. Describe how your culture and the people around you have influenced your values and identity.
  3. Identify your strengths and how they will support you during your learning journey.
  4. Evaluate your limitations in terms of your learning and career development. How might these affect your learning journey?
  5. Share an example of when you were trying to work productively with others, but there was resistance or tension. Discuss strategies you tried at the time, how effective they were, and your reflections on what other strategies you would try now and why.

The situation that involved an ethical decision was one where I was getting made redundant from my last job. I could have stayed and whiled away the time during the prolonged consultation, reassignment, and reappointment process; but none of the options that were presented before me led to me doing what I wanted to be doing and that was doing my job that was getting removed.

I really cared about job, I was excellent at it, and I enjoyed what I did - it really tickled my values of being able to help people, getting the correct information, and being needed. I felt like I mattered. It was obvious I did not matter as a person (both from the fact that I got made redundant and the fact that at my last day morning tea people only mentioned how much work I did and not who I was to them as a human), but what I did mattered and I did it well.

Being the expert at my last job became my identity. I knew a lot of things about a lot of things and for things I didn't know I could find the answers. People came to me from all parts of the organisation to ask for help. It really hurt when I had to make the choice that there would be an end to it. It was Sophie's choice really.

I know well what my strengths are and it's perseverance and commitment. Whenever I used to face work puzzles, I'd stick at it until I could figure it out. Many times I stayed up working on these well into the night. I often logged in on the weekends, and sometimes worked for 14 days straight. But I felt like it all mattered, because I could help other people solve their problems with my outputs.

But in that is also my weakness - I thought I mattered. I did not matter. Organisations have human resources departments for a reason - people are just cogs in the machine, easily replaceable with the next cog when it's needed. The machine keeps rolling. Especially big government organisations.

For this course, I want to try and change myself a little - care less, be less 'know-it-all', stick out less. There was a bit of a joke at my work when I'd go into a meeting and walk out being the only person with action points. I need to chill out and let other people speak more this time. I need to take it more slowly and enjoy the ride, rather than galloping towards the finish line.
