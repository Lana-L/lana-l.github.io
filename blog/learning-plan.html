Learning Plan

Time to formulate your Learning Plan!

The Learning Plan needs to include the following:

  1. What is your long term goal or career pathway?

    2. In terms of being a web developer, I'd like to get a job in a smallish team - where I can be given autonomous tasks, yet have people available to ask for help and guide me if needed. There are also several personal projects I'd like to finish and hopefully, if I can manage it, it would be a good contribution to my portfolio.

  2. A description of your strengths and limitations when it comes to learning

    3. I find it difficult to start from a blank page. For example, when I used to have a job writing Word documents, I never started from a blank page, I always used "save as" from a previous document. It didn't even seem to matter if I changed everything in the new document, but staring at a blank page left me paralysed with inaction. Even for this Sprint 2 blog challenge, I spent about two days doing absolutely nothing, and even considered dropping out because I couldn't begin.

    There was a trick I learned on TikTok recently and it was "allow yourself to half-arse things" or words to that effect. I use it often. So, in this instance, I was like "I'll just put some styles in and just at least get something to exist" and nek minit I've been at this for three hours and tweaking every single css value to make it look just right. It's a good trick, and I'm glad I remember to use it on myself.

  3. What skills (non-technical/human skills) would you like to see developed in yourself while at Dev Academy?

    4. I don't think it's something DevAcademy can really teach me, but I'd like to gain more creativity (in terms of artistry/design/ideas) and to be a bit more likeable. Both probably require a lot of introspection.

  4. A commitment to how you will manage your workload in this programme. This should include:
    • An explanation of how you will manage yourself to work productively and safely with other learners, Facilitators and industry/community representatives
    • What you will do when things are building up and starting to get to you

    Foundations is pretty chill, so I sort of work on it as the day goes by. Bootcamp will be harder and more time prescriptive. I will make sure to stick to the provided schedule.

    Will make sure to adhere to the values of integrity, kindness, and empathy so that all learners feel valued and included.

  5. A commitment as to how and where you will seek help in a timely way

    6. There are a few discord channels that are set up for help (foundations help desk and presumably the bootcamp will have another one). There also appears to be a wellbeing channel that may be helpful. Although I think I'd be too scared to ask for help there as it commits the issues to print and it appears the channels are not purged, so I don't really want all future dev academy cohorts reading about my problems.

    Sometimes just talking it out with friends or just in my own head may work.

  6. A description of what you expect from the Facilitation team

    7. I expect them to have friendly attitude, be knowledgeable about the material, and up to date with current trends/technologies. So far so good.

  7. Any scheduling information such as block-out times when you are committed to other things. For example, you might have whānau commitments like school drop-offs. It's important to plan these in.

    8. No other commitments for the foreseeable future. In the morning, I need to feed my pets, outside 'pets', and process some business orders, if there are any (usually I start this at 8:30 am and it can take anywhere from 30 min to two hours, depending if there are many orders, but with the bootcamp starting at 8:45, I will have to shift everything earlier, and if there are many orders I could deal with these at lunch time.)
